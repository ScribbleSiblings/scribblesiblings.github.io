  1. Intuitor

    Each night, learn the character (on or off script) that would help your team the most.

    Behind every great idea, behind every good thought, is intuition. Hone that, and maybe you too can think the right way.

    The Intuitor learns the most useful characters to their team, whether in play or not, or even on script or not.

    Examples

    The Intuitor learns Chef: Multiple evil characters are next to eachother.

    The Intuitor learns Barista: Critical good players are currently poisoned.

    The Intuitor learns Heretic: Good is on the brink of losing.

    How to Run

    Each night, wake the Intuitor, and show them the character token for the character you think would be most useful for their team.

  2. Priest

    Each night*, choose a player: if the Demon, you die. One good player registers as the Demon to your ability.

    Back, foul demon, BACK!

    The Priest dies if they encounter the Demon, or a specific good player.

    • There is always a good player that registers as the Demon, even if the original one changes alignment.

    Examples

    The Priest chooses the Po: they die.

    The Priest chooses the Chambermaid: nothing happens.

    The Priest chooses the Saint, who has been elected the Priest's Sinner: the Priest dies.

    How to Run

    On the first night, decide a good player to register incorrectly to the Priest - mark them "Sinner". Each night besides the first, wake the Priest, and have them choose a player. If that player is the Demon, or the player marked "Sinner", the Priest dies.

    Townsfolk

  3. Disruptor

    Executions fail if no Townsfolk voted. Evils know you are in play. [+0 or +1 Outsider]

    Nobody said it was *wrong* to sabotage things.

    The Disruptor forces Townsfolk to have the say by failing nominations with no Townsfolk votes.

    • Failed nominations have 0 votes.
    • All evil players know that the Disruptor is in play, even if they aren't Minions or Demons.
    • The Disruptor might add an Outsider, replacing a Townsfolk.

    Examples

    The High Priestess, Recluse, and Yaggababble voted. The vote count is 3, as normal, and at the end of the day, the nominee is executed.

    Only the Saint, Scarlet Woman, and Evil Twin voted on a nomination. The vote count is 0, but is announced as 3. At the end of the day, another player is about to die, so they are executed as normal.

    Both Outsiders, all 3 Minions, and the Demon are the only voters on a nomination. The vote count is 0, but is announced as 6. At the end of the day, that player would be about to die, so the previous about to die player is executed instead.

    How to Run

    On the first night, wake each evil player. Show them the "This character chose you" card, then the Disruptor token. Each nomination, if a Townsfolk voted, mark the Disruptor "Townsfolk Voted". When tallied, if the Disruptor is not marked "Townsfolk Voted", announce the player as about to die, but secretly they are not (the vote count is 0): mark the previous player "About to Die", to remember. At the end of the day, execute the player who was about to die.

  4. Groundskeeper

    If only evils live, they lose, even if you are dead.

    If none remain to tend the yard, it shall surely fall to ruin.

    The Groundskeeper forces evil to kill their own before the end of the game.

    • If at any point there are only evil players alive, good wins instantly.

    Examples

    The Imp kills the Undertaker, the last alive good player. Good wins.

    Only the Witch, Po, Evil Ogre, and the Evil Gnome live. Good wins.

    How to Run

    If the Groundskeeper is in play, and all living players are evil, good wins. Declare this and wrap up the game.

    Townsfolk

  5. Satanist

    Each night*, a player might die. You & Minions think you are (and you have the ability of) an evil Minion. You are "the Demon". [+1 Minion, No Demon]

    In the forgotten corners, in the darkest nooks, so does live the evil amongst us.

    The Satanist makes 2 players who think they are a Minion, but adds paranoia to one being secretly good.

    • If the Satanist is in play, the Demon and Satanist tokens are replaced with Minions.
    • The Satanist still registers as a good Townsfolk to abilities.
    • If the Satanist dies, good wins (including them).

    Examples

    In a 15 player game, 5 players think they are MInions, and have no Demon.

    In a 9 player game, 3 players think they are Minions. The Poisoner is secretly the Satanist, and when they die, good wins (including them).

    How to Run

    During setup, replace the Demon and the Satanist tokens with Minions. Mark one of the Minions "Is the Satanist" - they are "The Demon", and secretly good.
    During the Minion info phase, show the Satanist as a Minion, and show the Satanist token instead of a Demon.
    Each night besides the first, choose a player to kill (or not to kill).

  6. Blackout

    An alive good player is drunk, even if you are dead. Each day, guess who it is: if right, you learn a true statement & are drunk until dawn.

    Lot o' good that Soldier trainin' did ya! *I'm drunk!*

    The Blackout causes an alive good player to be drunk. However, each day they can guess it, they learn a true fact.

    • The Blackout means a good player is drunk, even if the Blackout is themselves dead.
    • If the drunk player dies, an alive good player becomes drunk instead.
    • Each day, the Blackout can guess who is drunk - if right, they learn a true statement, and are then drunk until dawn.
    • The Blackout cannot guess whilst dead, but still makes a player drunk.

    Examples

    The Empath is drunk. The Blackout tells the Storyteller they think the Fortune Teller is drunk, and learn nothing.

    The Puzzlemaster is drunk. When they die, the General becomes drunk instead.

    The Alchemist is drunk. The Blackout tells the Storyteller they think the Alchemist is drunk, and learns the Demon is 3 steps from their nearest Minion. That night, the Alchemist's assasin kill goes through. The next day, they say the same, and learn that the Demon is an odd number of seats from them.

    How to Run

    On the first night, choose a good player to be drunk - mark them "Drunk". When that player dies, choose another. Each day, allow the Blackout to speak with you privately to name another player: if it is the player marked "Drunk", the Blackout is drunk until dawn, and whisper them a true statement - mark them "Correct", remove it at Dawn. Otherwise, send them away.

  7. Plagued

    Townsfolk that vote on your execution are drunk until tomorrow dusk, even if you are dead.

    Don't touch me!

    Townsfolk that vote for the Plagued's execution are drunk until the next dusk.

    • If a Townsfolk votes for the Plagued's execution, they are instantly drunk.
    • Players drunk this way are drunk for the rest of the day, the next night, and the whole of the day after.

    Examples

    The Empath votes for the Plagued. That night, despite having an evil neighbour, they learn 0.

    The Slayer votes for the Plagued. The next day, they choose the Demon, but nothing happens.

    How to Run

    Each day, if the Plagued is nominated and any Townsfolk vote for the execution, they are drunk - mark them "Drunk". Each dusk, remove "Drunk" marks not placed today.

    Outsider

  8. Ra'atchet

    On your 1st night, choose a direction: players register as their neighbour 1 step in that direction to Townsfolk abilities.

    etB ouy ookedl pu hatw hist eantm.

    The Ra'atchet rotates the grimoire one step for Townsfolk abilities.

    • The Ra'atchet must choose a direction.
    • All players register as their neighbour in that direction to Townsfolk abilities.
    • Any choices at night Townsfolk make instead choose the neighbour of the person they chose.
    • Characters that learn passive information are also affected by this, learning instead about players 1 step over.

    Examples

    The Fortune Teller chooses the Demon and the Saint, their Red Herring. However, because the Ra'atchet registered each as their neighbour, they learn No.

    The Saint is executed. Good loses.

    The Gambler guesses themself as the Gambler, and their clockwise neighbour dies.

    The Spy learns the entire grimoire rotated 1 step clockwise.

    The Ra'atchet chooses Clockwise. That night, the Empath learns a 1 despite both their neighbours being good, as the player 2 steps clockwise from them is evil.

    The Ra'atchet chooses Anticlockwise. The Slayer shoots the Demon's anticlockwise neighbour, and the Demon dies.

    How to Run

    On the first night, wake the Ra'atchet and let them indicate a direction - place the "Clockwise" or "Anti-Clockwise" in the centre of the Grimoire to remind you. Whenever a Townsfolk ability would learn information about or affect in any way a player, it instead affects their neighbour 1 step in the marked direction.

    This is quite hard to run.

  9. Archeolos

    You start by choosing another alive player to give this ability. This is your only ability (if Townsfolk), or this acts twice (if evil & given by good).

    Ok class, today we're going to see what these do. Catch!

    The Archeolos forces good players to play hot potato.

    • Townsfolk with the Archeolos ability have no other ability until they lose the Archeolos ability.
    • If a good player gives the ability to an evil player, that evil player can give out 2 copies of the ability.

    Examples

    The Archeolos chooses the Fortune Teller. That night, the Fortune Teller isn't woken for their ability. They then choose the Soldier; when the Demon chooses them later that night, the Soldier dies.

    The Saint with the Archeolos ability chooses the Po. The next night, the Po chooses the Empath and the Spy.

    How to Run

    Each night, wake all players with the Archeolos ability one by one, and get them to choose an alive player that isn't themselves: mark the new player "Has Ability", and if they are evil and the original player is good, mark them "Doubled". If they were marked "Doubled", make them choose twice.

    If more players have the ability than reminder tokens, use a notebook and pen.

  10. Sorceror

    Townsfolk nominations poison good nominees. Each night*, if none did, choose a player: they are poisoned until dusk.

    To turn upon one's own must be punished.

    The Sorceror forces good to permanently poison other good players, or face a night of untrustworthy information.

    • If a Townsfolk nominates a good player, that player is poisoned.
    • Townsfolk or Outsiders can be poisoned, but only Townsfolk nominations can cause the poison.
    • If nobody became poisoned because of the Sorceror in a day, they choose a player that night, who is poisoned until dusk.

    Examples

    The Choirboy nominates the King. The King is poisoned.

    The Fortune Teller nominates the Snitch. The Snitch is poisoned.

    No Townsfolk nominated today. Tonight, the Sorceror wakes, and chooses the Fortune Teller: they choose the Saint and Empath, neither their red herring, but learn a yes.

    How to Run

    Each day, if a Townsfolk nominates a good player, that player is poisoned, and mark the Sorceror "Townsfolk Nominated". Each night besides the first, if the Sorceror is not marked "Townsfolk Nominated", wake them and let them choose a player. That player is poisoned until dusk, mark them "Chosen".

  11. Siren

    Each night*, choose a player: they die. Outsiders you kill change alignment. [Outsiders might start evil, -1 to +1 Outsider]

    They say you oughta dance like no one's watching
    They're a telepathic chorus in my mind

    The Siren makes some Outsiders evil, and changes their alignment upon killing them.

    • The Siren chooses a player to kill each night besides the first.
    • The Siren forces there to be 1 more or 1 fewer Outsider than normal.
    • Outsiders might start evil, and learn if they are on the 1st night.
    • If the Siren kills an Outsider, they change alignment from good to evil or vice versa.

    Examples

    There should be 1 Outsider, but both the Saint and Zealot are in play. The Zealot is evil.

    When the Siren chooses the evil Recluse, they die and become good.

    How to Run

    Upon setup, you may add or remove one Outsider token from the bag, and decide which Outsiders are evil - wake them on the first night to inform them. Each night besides the first, waken the Siren, and let them choose a player - that player dies. If that player was an Outsider and they died, change their alignment and wake them to tell them their new alignment.

  12. Destroyer

    Each night*, you may choose a player & a direction: They & 2 players in that direction for every night you didn't kill die.

    Goodbye.

    The Destroyer can choose to hold off to kill more, but a night's kills must be in a line.

    • When the Destroyer chooses the kill, they choose a player and the direction for the line of kills instead of each player individually.
    • The Destroyer gains 2 extra kills per night they chose not to kill.

    Examples

    On the second night, the Destroyer chooses Clockwise and the Fortune Teller: they die.

    On the second night, the Destroyer chooses to hold. On the third night, they choose Clockwise and the Empath: the Empath and their 2 Clockwise neighbours die.

    The Destroyer has held for 3 nights. They choose their counterclockwise neighbour, the Alchemist, and counterclockwise. The Alchemist, and their 6 anticlockwise neighbours die.

    How to Run

    Place the "Kill Length" marker on the Destroyer. Each night besides the first, wake them, and allow them to choose a player and indicate a direction, or shake their head. If they shake their head, rotate the "Kill Length" marker two steps clockwise. If they choose a player and direction, note how far the "Kill Length" marker is from the Destroyer: the chosen player and that many players in the chosen direction die. Then, place the "Kill Length" marker back on the Destroyer.

  13. Wrazcal

    Each night*, choose a player: learn how helpful killing them is to your team. Then, choose if they die. If not, tomorrow night, instead choose 2 player: they die.

    To kill or not to kill? I do so enjoy toying with my prey, but I get ever so hungry...

    The Wrazcal has the choice between an informed kill or two kills the next night.

    • The Wrazcal learns how good their kill would be when they choose.
    • They may then choose to follow through with the kill, or hold off.
    • If they hold off, they may not choose again, but instead choose two players to kill tomorrow night.
    • If the Wrazcal chooses 2 players, they don't learn how good those kills are.

    Examples

    The Wrazcal chooses the Savant. They learn 4, and choose to kill them.

    The Wrazcal chooses the Saint. They learn 2, and choose to kill them.

    The Wrazcal chooses the Moonchild, who is Innnkeeper protected. They learn 0, and choose to hold off. Tomorrow night, they choose the Innkeeper and Pacifist, who both die.

    How to Run

    Each night besides the first, wake the Wrazcal. If they are marked "Twice", remove it, and let them choose 2 players: they die. Otherwise, let them choose a single player. Inform them from 0-5 how helpful killing that player is for the Wrazcal's team, then allow them to nod or shake their head. If they nod, that player dies. If they shake, mark them "Twice", and put them to sleep.

  14. Kuthullion

    Each night*, see the grimoire, then choose a player: they die. After day 3, if all evils live, they lose.

    I have seen the dark universe yawning
    Where the black planets roll without aim,
    Where they roll in their horror unheeded,
    Without knowledge, or lustre, or name.

    The Kuthullion knows the grimoire before they kill, but must kill an evil by the 4th night.

    • The Kuthullion sees the grimoire before they kill, but only from the second night onwards.
    • If no evil players are dead by the 4th night, good wins.

    Examples

    The Kuthullion sees the Undertaker has been hiding as the Recluse, and kills them.

    At the end of the 3rd day, the Scarlet Woman is dead. The game continues.

    At the end of the 3rd day, no evil players are dead. Good wins.

    How to Run

    At the end of the day, if the Kuthullion is unmarked, mark them "1st Day". If marked "1st Day", remove it and mark them "2nd Day". If marked "2nd Day", check if all evil players are alive: if so, good wins.
    Each night besides the first, wake the Kuthullion, and show them the grimoire. Let them choose a player, that player dies.

  15. Ketacles

    Each night*, choose a player: they die. If 3 players live, good only wins if an Outsider is executed. [+1 Outsider]

    My roots take hold in the minds of those scorned.

    The Ketacles forces good to execute an Outsider on the final day to win.

    • If the Ketacles is in play, an extra Outsider is added to the bag.
    • On the final day, good wins if and only if an Outsider is executed.
    • Good still loses even if they execute the Ketacles on the final day, or if they do not execute.
    • Good wins if they execute an outsider, regardless of if that Outsider is dead or alive.

    Examples

    3 players live, and nobody is executed. Evil wins immediately.

    3 players live, and the Ketacles is executed. Evil wins.

    3 players live, and the alive Barber is executed. Good wins.

    3 players live, and the dead Klutz is executed. Good wins.

  16. Skemator

    Evil wins if noone is executed in a day, or after day 3, extended by 1 for each alive Minion.

    Those who serve me only weigh me down. Come, see how you fare with us both alone.

    The Skemator wins earlier if its Minions are killed before it.

    • The Skemator has a threshold for winning at day (3 + alive Minions).
    • If this is ever passed, evil wins.
    • If an alive Minion is killed the day evil would win after, evil wins instantly.
    • If an alive Minion is executed the day before evil would win, evil still wins instantly, as the timer reduces to the end of the current day.
    • If nobody is executed each day, evil wins.

    Examples

    There are 2 alive Minions, and day 5 ends with a good player's execution: evil wins.

    It is day 3, and the only Minion is executed: evil wins.

    It is day 4, with only 1 alive Minion. They are nominated by the Golem, die, and evil wins.

    It is day 3, with no alive Minions. The Skemator is executed, and good wins.

    On the 1st day, nobody is executed. Evil wins.

    On the 2nd day, the Sailor is executed and survives. The game continues.

  18. The House

    Players may make wagers with eachother or the Storyteller, they are binding if the Storyteller agrees. If you win a wager, something good happens for your team (you learn what).

    Don't bet against the house.

    The House allows wagers, and gets rewarded extra if they win.

    • If The House is in play, players may make wagers.
    • Each player in the wager sets a condition, and a price and/or reward.
    • If the Storyteller hears the terms and agrees, it is binding, and the price/reward will come true should they be needed.
    • If the House wins a wager, something good happens for their team, and they learn it.

    Examples

    The Empath wagers they learn a 2 tonight. They state that if they win, they're sober, otherwise they're drunk. The Storyteller agrees, and they become drunk when they learn a 1.

    The Goblin wagers with the Fortune Teller they won't be executed if they claim Goblin, on condition that the loser is drunk immediately. The Goblin is executed, becomes drunk, and doesn't win.

    The good The House wagers with the High Priestess that 2 players will die tonight, and the loser gives their vote token to the winner. 2 players die that night, so the High Priestess' vote token is given to the House, and The House learns that the drunk Village Idiot became sober.

  19. Temptation

    Each night, choose a player: if evil, they learn this. If they nominate you tomorrow, they are drunk. If the Demon, a Minion is instead.

    ...That's not a flower. Order all the employees nearby to evacuate immediately.

    Temptation forces evils they choose to being wary of nominating, for fear of drunkenness.

    • Temptation chooses a player each night, regardless.
    • Evil players that Temptation choose learn they were chosen, but not by whom.
    • Regardless of alignment, if the chosen player nominates Temptation tomorrow, they are drunk.
    • Players drunk from Temptation remain drunk even if Temptation is dead.

    Examples

    Temptation chooses the Alsaahir. The Alsaahir nominates Temptation, and is drunk from then on.

    Temptation chooses the Assassin. The Assassin learns they were chosen, is cautious about nominating tomorrow, and does not end up drunk.

    Temptation chooses the Imp. The Imp gambles they won't nominate wrong, nominates Temptation, and becomes drunk. The Assassin kills Temptation, but the Imp is still drunk.

    How to Run

    Each night, let Temptation choose a player. Mark them "Tempted". If they are evil, wake them, show them "This character chose you", and Temptation's token.
    Each day, if the player marked "Tempted" nominates Temptation, they are drunk - mark them "Drunk".

  20. Blossoms

    Each night, choose a player. Every second time a player you chose was executed, learn the alignments of the nominators.

    The more blood it has, the more beautiful it is

    Blossoms learns the alignments of those who feed it with its chosen victims.

    • Every night, Blossoms chooses a player.
    • Every 2nd time a player Blossoms chose is executed, Blossoms wakes that night to learn the alignments of the nominators.
    • They learn both alignments in an arbitrary order.
    • On the night they learn information, Blossoms still chooses a player.

    Examples

    Blossoms chooses the Snake Charmer, the Gambler, and the Recluse over the first 3 nights. On the 2nd day, the Gambler is executed, and on the 3rd, the Snake Charmer is. That night, Blossoms wakes to learn the alignments of their nominators, and to choose the Empath.

    The Undertaker is executed by the Scarlet Woman, and the good Goon is executed by the Lunatic, both having been chosen. Blossoms wakes and learns Good and Evil.

    Blossoms learns information on the 3rd night, but chooses more players who are executed, and so learns information on the 5th night too.

    How to Run

    Each night, wake Blossoms and let them choose a player: mark them "Chosen". If Blossoms has two "Good Nominator"/"Evil Nominator" marks, remove them, and inform them the alignments respectively.
    If a player marked "Chosen" is executed, mark Blossoms with "Good Nominator" or "Evil Nominator", according to the alignment of the nominator.

  21. Recklessness

    Each night, choose a player. If they are evil & you have chosen an evil player before, lose this ability.

    Life is only granted to those who hold no fear of death.

    Recklessness looses its ability after choosing evil players twice.

    • Recklessness does not learn anything upon choosing a player.
    • After the 2nd time Recklessness chooses an evil player, they don't wake from now on.

    Examples

    Recklessness chooses the Empath, then the good Cult Leader, then the Godfather, then the Summoner, and doesn't wake from then on.

    Recklessness chooses the Scarlet Woman on the 1st and 2nd nights, and doesn't wake from then on.

    Recklessness chooses the Snake Charmer twice in a row. They then choose the Imp twice, and stop waking.

    How to Run

    Each night, wake Recklessness and allow them to choose a player. If they chose evil, and are unmarked, mark them "Once", if already marked "Once" mark them "No Ability".

    Townsfolk

  22. Longing

    Each night, choose 2 living players (not yourself): learn which the Storyteller believes would be better for their team to execute tomorrow.

    I am coming to you. You, who will be reduced to ash like me.

    Longing learns which of two players would be better for their allies to sacrifice.

    • Longing chooses 2 players each night, and learns 1.
    • Longing learns which of the 2 players they chose the Storyteller believes is better for the player's allies to execute tomorrow.
    • Longing only learns about tomorrow's execution, not future days.
    • Longing learns about how it would be for the player's allies to execute them, which may be a different team for each player chosen.
    • Longing may not choose themselves.

    Examples

    Longing chooses the Fortune Teller and the Washerwoman, and learns the Washerwoman player.

    Longing chooses the Baron and the Saint, and learns the Baron.

    Longing chooses the Imp and the Professor, and learns the Imp - the evil team has a Mastermind that nobody has thought of, meanwhile the Professor has yet to use their ability.

    How to Run

    Each night, wake Longing, and allow them to choose 2 players. Show them which you believe would be better for that player's allies to execute tomorrow.

  23. Noise

    Each night, choose a unique player (not yourself): learn a character. It's in play if & only if they are evil.

    This is a recording of the day we must never forget.

    Noise learns out of play characters, unless they choose an evil player.

    • Noise learns a character each night.
    • That character is out of play, unless the player they chose was evil.

    Examples

    Noise chooses the Empath and sees the Fang Gu token.

    Noise chooses the Scarlet Woman and sees the Scarlet Woman token.

    How to Run

    Each night, wake Noise, and let them choose a player not marked "Chosen" - mark their choice "Chosen" (or note down if needed). If they chose a good player, show an out of play character, otherwise show an in play character.

  24. Emotion

    Each night, choose a player (not yourself): tomorrow, you may visit the Storyteller to learn a fact; if they are evil, learn false info.

    It's a good day! Are you still shy today?

    Emotion learns a statement as true as the heart of the player they choose.

    • Emotion chooses a player each night.
    • This initially does nothing, until they visit the Storyteller, and learn a statement.
    • They learn a true statement if they chose a good player, and a false one if they chose an evil player.

    Examples

    Emotion chooses the Klutz. That day, they visit the Storyteller and learn "Your choice neighbours a Minion." - this is true.

    Emotion chooses the Marionette. That day, they visit the Storyteller and learn "The Demon is a woman." - the Demon is non-binary; Emotion learned false information.

    How to Run

    Each night, wake Emotion, and allow them to choose a player. If they chose evil, mark them "False Info".
    If Emotion comes to speak with you, whisper a true statement to them, or a false one if they're marked "False Info".

  25. Aspiration

    Each night*, learn the alignment of the nominator of the previous player you learnt about (yourself if there was none).

    • Once a statement has been made, everyone votes on if they agree, starting with Echoes' clockwise neighbour.
    • Players vote whether alive or dead, and dead players don't need a vote token.
    • The night after they declare, Echoes learns if a strict majority of players were correct.
    • However, if a strict majority of evil players voted, Echoes learns false information.

    Examples

    Echoes declares that the Demon neighbours a Minion. The majority vote they agree, and the majority of evils vote they disagree. It is true, so Echoes learns yes that night.

    Echoes declares that the Virgin, whose ability executed someone, is the Virgin. This is an invalid statement.

    Echoes declares that their neighbours both could wake that night. The majority votes they disagree, but evils vote they agree. As both could, and evils agreed, Echoes learns yes.

    Echoes declares that precisely 2 characters in play have "You start knowing" in their text. 7 players vote either way, and so Echoes doesn't wake that night.

    Each day, allow Echoes to declare a statement - mark them "Called". Run a vote as for an exile, where players raise their hand if they think the statement is true (if needed, ask them to keep their hands raised). When the vote concludes, note if the strict majority was right, and if the strict majority of the evils was right (mark Echoes "Majority Correct" and "Evils Correct"). If there was an even split, nothing happens.
    Each night besides the first, if Echoes is marked "Called", remove it and wake them. Inform them if the majority was correct, but give them false info if evils was correct.
